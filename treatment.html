Treatment Guidelines

Standard treatment protocols based on diagnosis. These are for informational purposes only and should be administered by a qualified healthcare professional.

Malaria Treatment

Standard Drug Administration (All Cases)

  • Primary Drug: Artemether-lumefantrine (Coartem) is a common and effective combination therapy.
  • Alternative: Artesunate-amodiaquine.
  • Dosage is determined by patient weight and age. Must be prescribed by a doctor.

Additional Procedures (for Very Strong Signs)

  • If the patient presents with Very Strong Signs (e.g., severe vomiting, abdominal pain), a Chest X-ray is recommended to rule out complications like pneumonia.
  • Intravenous (IV) fluids may be required for dehydration.
  • Close monitoring of vital signs is essential.
Typhoid Fever Treatment

Standard Drug Administration (All Cases)

  • Primary Antibiotic: Fluoroquinolones like Ciprofloxacin are often used.
  • Alternative: Azithromycin or Ceftriaxone, especially in areas with antibiotic resistance.
  • A full course of antibiotics is crucial to prevent relapse.

Additional Procedures (for Very Strong Signs)

  • If the patient presents with Very Strong Signs (e.g., severe abdominal pain, sore throat), a Chest X-ray is recommended to check for intestinal perforation or pneumonia.
  • Supportive care, including fluid and electrolyte replacement, is vital.
Tuberculosis (TB) Treatment

Standard Drug Administration

  • TB requires a long course of multiple antibiotics, typically for 6 months or more.
  • Common Drugs: Isoniazid, Rifampin, Ethambutol, and Pyrazinamide.
  • Treatment must be strictly followed as prescribed to prevent drug resistance.
Diabetes Management

Lifestyle and Medication

  • Lifestyle: Diet control, regular exercise, and weight management are fundamental.
  • Oral Medication: Metformin is a common first-line treatment for Type 2 Diabetes.
  • Insulin Therapy: Required for all Type 1 Diabetes patients and some Type 2 patients.
  • Regular blood sugar monitoring is essential.
