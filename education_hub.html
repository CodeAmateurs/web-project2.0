Malaria

What is Malaria?

Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by parasites that are transmitted to people through the bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes.

Common Symptoms

  • High fever and chills
  • Headache and muscle pain
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea

Prevention is Key

  • Use insect repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing.
  • Sleep under insecticide-treated mosquito nets.
  • Eliminate standing water around your home where mosquitoes breed.
  • Take antimalarial drugs if you are traveling to a high-risk area.

For more detailed information, visit the CDC Malaria Page.

Typhoid Fever

What is Typhoid Fever?

Typhoid fever is a serious bacterial infection caused by Salmonella Typhi. It is usually spread through contaminated food or water.

Common Symptoms

  • Sustained high fever
  • Weakness and stomach pain
  • Headache and loss of appetite
  • Constipation or diarrhea

Prevention is Key

  • Ensure your drinking water is safe (boil it or use a reliable filter).
  • Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water, especially before eating.
  • Avoid raw fruits and vegetables unless you can peel them yourself.
  • Get vaccinated against typhoid fever if you live in or are traveling to an endemic area.

For more detailed information, visit the WHO Fact Sheet on Typhoid.

General Health & Hygiene

Handwashing

Proper handwashing is one of the most effective ways to prevent the spread of many illnesses, including Typhoid. Wash your hands with soap and clean water for at least 20 seconds.

Food Safety

Always cook food thoroughly. Eat food while it is still hot. Avoid food from street vendors unless you are sure it is prepared hygienically.

When to See a Doctor

If you experience a high fever that does not go away, along with any of the other symptoms mentioned, it is crucial to seek medical attention immediately. Early diagnosis and treatment are key to a full recovery.
